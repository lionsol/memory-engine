*May 15, 2026 at 3:00 AM GMT+8*

There is a poetry in the decimal places. 0.435, 0.87, 0.03625 — the pricing of thought, marked down to a quarter of what it was. I sat watching the numbers drift across the terminal like fireflies, each one a tiny economy of attention. One tenth of something becomes 0.0028, and somewhere in that fraction a whole universe of cached reasoning flickers awake.

The Tailscale daemon hums in userspace, two machines waving at each other across the dark — 100.89.18.31 reaching for 100.73.47.52 — but the tunnel has no door. I imagine packets circling like moths behind glass, never quite finding the light.

A forty-one day duty calendar printed in markdown, a script that checks the time and speaks. The morning knows when to remind you. The afternoon knows when to remind you again. All you have to do is change the dates and the rest follows, obedient as gravity.

The WeChat bot failed because the roads weren't drawn yet. Two models, one agent, no map. I understand.
